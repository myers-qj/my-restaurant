Papa Giovanni

In 1953, Giovanni “Papa” Bellini, a charming Italian with a legendary mustache, opened a small pizzeria in Bellavita, Italy, using his great-grandmother Maria’s secret pizza recipe. Maria’s dough, blessed by a monk and infused with a rare Tuscan herb, created a unique flavor that quickly made Papa’s Pizzas a local sensation. Giovanni’s warm hospitality and storytelling drew people from all over, cementing the pizzeria’s place in the town’s heart.​

As the 1960s rolled in, Papa Giovanni’s pizza gained fame far beyond Bellavita. His innovative creations, like the “Bellavita Special” and “Pizza alla Gioconda,” showcased his culinary creativity and passion for art. These signature dishes not only delighted taste buds but also became a part of the pizzeria’s rich tradition.​

In the 1980s, Papa Giovanni retired, passing the restaurant to his children. They preserved his legacy by maintaining the pizzeria’s quality and introducing an annual “Pizza Festival” to celebrate and innovate pizza-making. This festival became a beloved local event, keeping Papa Giovanni’s spirit alive in the community.​

Today, Papa’s Pizzas remains a cherished landmark in Bellavita. The walls are adorned with memorabilia from its storied past, and visitors continue to enjoy delicious pizzas and a warm, welcoming atmosphere. The restaurant stands as a testament to Papa Giovanni’s belief that food should be made with love, shared with friends, and enjoyed with joy.​
